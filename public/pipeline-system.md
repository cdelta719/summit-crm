## Stage 6: NEGOTIATION

**Definition:** They want to move forward but are pushing back on terms, price, scope, or timeline.

**Entry Criteria:** Prospect has responded to proposal with questions, requests for changes, or price pushback.

**Exit Criteria:** Agreement reached (Stage 7) OR deal lost (Stage 8).

**Required Actions:**
- [ ] Listen to their specific concerns (don't assume)
- [ ] Identify what they actually want changed vs. what's a negotiating tactic
- [ ] Respond within 24 hours to every negotiation touchpoint
- [ ] Use the flexibility guide below
- [ ] Get everything agreed upon in writing

**Time Limit:** Negotiations shouldn't exceed 7 days. If they're dragging, something else is going on â€” uncover it.

**What You CAN Flex On:**
- Payment terms (split payments, net 30 on second half)
- Onboarding timeline (delay start date if they need time)
- Scope of initial setup (start smaller, add later)
- Additional training sessions for their team
- Extended pilot period (7 days â†’ 14 days for Enterprise)
- Annual contract discount (15% off monthly if paid yearly)

**What You CANNOT Flex On:**
- Setup fee below $4,000 (Growth) or $12,000 (Enterprise) â€” hold the line
- Monthly managed care fee â€” this is your recurring revenue, protect it
- Scope creep without price adjustment â€” more integrations = more money
- Free work as a "test" â€” free pilots are structured (see Competitive Edge Playbook)
- Your team's time boundaries â€” don't let negotiations eat your selling hours

**If they ask for a discount:**
*"I appreciate you asking. The price reflects the value â€” and we've built the ROI case that shows [X]x return. What I can do is offer a payment plan to make it easier on cash flow: $2,500 now, $2,500 in 30 days. Or if you commit to an annual contract on the monthly service, I can do 15% off â€” that's $212/month instead of $250. Which of those works better?"*

# SECTION 5: WEEKLY SALES RHYTHM

## The Weekly Playbook

### MONDAY â€” Pipeline Review & Planning Day

**9:00 - 10:00 AM: Pipeline Review**
- Open CRM / Google Sheet
- Review every active deal:
  - What stage is it in?
  - What's the next action?
  - Is it stalled? Why?
  - Does the lead score still make sense?
- Identify the top 3 deals most likely to close this week
- Identify any deals that need to be moved to Closed Lost or Nurture

**10:00 - 10:30 AM: Weekly Planning**
- Set the week's targets:
  - How many calls will you make?
  - How many discoveries will you book?
  - How many proposals will you send?
  - Which deals will you close?
- Block calendar: protect 9-11 AM and 2-4 PM Tues-Thurs for calls
- Prep any research needed for Tuesday's calls

### TUESDAY-THURSDAY â€” Execution Days

**9:00 - 11:00 AM: Power Dial Block #1**
- This is sacred time. No meetings. No email. No distractions.
- Work through your call list: hottest leads first
- Target: 15-25 dials per block
- Log every outcome immediately

**11:00 AM - 12:00 PM: Follow-ups & Admin**
- Send follow-up emails from morning calls
- Update CRM
- Respond to inbound leads (within 5 minutes!)
- Prepare for afternoon demos/discoveries

**12:00 - 1:00 PM: Lunch + LinkedIn**
- Send 3-5 LinkedIn connection requests
- Comment on 2-3 posts from prospects
- Share or post content (see Content Engine in Competitive Edge Playbook)

**2:00 - 4:00 PM: Discoveries, Demos, & Follow-Up Calls**
- This block is for scheduled calls, demos, and warm follow-ups
- Between calls: send proposals, prep for next call

**4:00 - 5:00 PM: Wrap-Up**
- Update CRM with all activities
- Send any outstanding follow-ups
- Prep for tomorrow's calls

### FRIDAY â€” Review & Prep Day

**9:00 - 10:00 AM: Weekly Review**
- How many calls did you make this week?
- How many conversations? Discoveries booked? Proposals sent?
- What closed? What moved forward? What stalled?
- Win/loss analysis: what worked, what didn't?

**10:00 - 11:00 AM: Prep Next Week**
- Build next week's call list
- Research new prospects
- Update prospect list in CRM
- Identify any scripts/approaches to refine

**11:00 AM - 12:00 PM: Learning & Improvement**
- Review one lost deal: what could you have done differently?
- Practice one objection handling response
- Read one article about your target industries
- Refine one script or template

### MONTHLY â€” Strategic Review

**First Monday of Each Month (60 minutes):**
- Pipeline health: total value, stage distribution, velocity
- Win/loss analysis: what are the patterns?
- Revenue vs. target: are you on track?
- Conversion rates between stages: where's the bottleneck?
- Script audit: are scripts still working? What needs updating?
- Competitive landscape: any new threats or opportunities?
- Set next month's targets
